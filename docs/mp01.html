Introduction

In 2023, the US Supreme Court ruled in Students for Fair Admissions v. Harvard (SFFA) that race-conscious practices in college admission processes were unconstitutional and effectively outlawed affirmative action in college admissions. Last year, the Integrated Postsecondary Education Data System (IPEDS), a federally managed data system, released the first batch of college enrollment data since the SFFA ruling was handed down.

In this mini-project, I intend to summarize and explore the federal demography data provided in IPEDS to compare the most recent year-over-year statistics for 2024 enrollment with historical trends to draw initial insights. From this, I will complete an analysis of the possible effects on student demographics when affirmative action is taken out of the equation. The main question I seek to ask and answer is “Did affirmative action programs help, hinder or not at all affect college admissions?”.

To do this, I employ dplyr for tabular data analysis and present my results on a web page created with quarto. Finally, I will describe the impact of SFFA on admitted student demographics at my alma mater, University of Maryland - College Park, from the perspective of the university president writing an op-ed for the UMD school newspaper, The Diamondback.

Data Acquisition and Preparation

Data Acquisition from IPEDS

Data is acquired from IPEDS and a data frame called IPEDS is created with fall enrollment and institution description data, which will be used for any analysis in this project. The data is further cleaned and prepared to identify enrollment groups by race/ethnicity and gender while institutions are identify institution names, ids and states.

Display Code 
#' IPEDS Data for MP#01 - Data starts in 2010
#' This function will acquire and standardize all data for MP#01
#' from IPEDS (https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/datacenter/DataFiles.aspx)

acquire_ipeds_data <- function(start_year=2010, end_year=2024){
    library(tidyverse)
    library(glue)
    
    data_dir <- file.path("data", "mp01")
    
    if(!dir.exists(data_dir)){
        dir.create(data_dir, showWarnings=FALSE, recursive=TRUE)
    }
    
    YEARS <- seq(start_year, end_year)
    
    EFA_ALL <- map(YEARS, function(yy){
        if(yy <= 2022){
            ef_url <- glue("https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/datacenter/data/EF{yy}A.zip")
            
        } else {
            ef_url <- glue("https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/data-generator?year={yy}&tableName=EF{yy}A&HasRV=0&type=csv")
        }
        
        ef_file <- file.path(data_dir, glue("ef{yy}a.csv.zip"))
        
        if(!file.exists(ef_file)){
            message(glue("Downloading Enrollment Data for {yy} from {ef_url}"))
            download.file(ef_url, destfile = ef_file, quiet=TRUE, mode="wb")    
        }
        
        read_csv(ef_file, 
                 show_col_types=FALSE) |>
            mutate(year = yy, 
                   # American Indian or Alaskan Native
                   enrollment_m_aian = EFAIANM, 
                   enrollment_f_aian = EFAIANW, 
                   # Asian
                   enrollment_m_asia = EFASIAM, 
                   enrollment_f_asia = EFASIAW, 
                   # Black or African-American, 
                   enrollment_m_bkaa = EFBKAAM, 
                   enrollment_f_bkaa = EFBKAAW, 
                   # Hispanic 
                   enrollment_m_hisp = EFHISPM, 
                   enrollment_f_hisp = EFHISPW, 
                   # Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander 
                   enrollment_m_nhpi = EFNHPIM, 
                   enrollment_f_nhpi = EFNHPIW, 
                   # White
                   enrollment_m_whit = EFWHITM, 
                   enrollment_f_whit = EFWHITW, 
                   # Two or More Races
                   enrollment_m_2mor = EF2MORM, 
                   enrollment_f_2mor = EF2MORW, 
                   # Unknown / Undisclosed Race
                   enrollment_m_unkn = EFUNKNM, 
                   enrollment_f_unkn = EFUNKNW, 
                   # US Non-Resident
                   enrollment_m_nral = EFNRALM, 
                   enrollment_f_nral = EFNRALW, 
            ) |> filter(
                (EFALEVEL %in% c(2, 12)) | (LINE %in% c(1, 15))
                # Per 2024 Data Dictionary, 
                # - EFALEVEL 2 = undergrad 
                # - EFALELVE 12 = grad
                # - Line 1 = first year first time full-time undergrad
                # - Line 15 = first year first time part-time undergrad
            ) |> mutate(level = case_when(
                       EFALEVEL == 2 ~ "all undergrad", 
                       EFALEVEL == 12 ~ "all graduate",
                       LINE %in% c(1, 15) ~ "first year undergrad"
                   )
            ) |>
            select(institution_id = UNITID, 
                   year, 
                   level,
                   starts_with("enrollment_")) |>
            group_by(institution_id, 
                     year, 
                     level) |>
            summarize(across(starts_with("enrollment_"), sum), 
                      .groups = "drop")
        
    }) |> bind_rows()
    
    DESC_ALL <- map(YEARS, function(yy){
        if(yy <= 2022){
            hd_url <- glue("https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/datacenter/data/HD{yy}.zip")
            
        } else {
            hd_url <- glue("https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/data-generator?year={yy}&tableName=HD{yy}&HasRV=0&type=csv")
        }
        
        hd_file <- file.path(data_dir, glue("hd{yy}.csv.zip"))
        
        if(!file.exists(hd_file)){
            message(glue("Downloading Institutional Descriptions for {yy} from {hd_url}"))
            download.file(hd_url, destfile = hd_file, quiet=TRUE, mode="wb")    
        }
        
        suppressWarnings(
            read_csv(hd_file, 
                 show_col_types=FALSE, 
                 locale=locale(encoding=if_else(yy==2024, "utf-8", "windows-1252"))) |>
            mutate(year = yy, 
                   INSTNM) |> 
            select(institution_id = UNITID, 
                   institution_name = INSTNM, 
                   state = STABBR, 
                   year)
        )
        
    }) |> bind_rows()
    
    inner_join(EFA_ALL, 
               DESC_ALL, 
               join_by(institution_id == institution_id, 
                       year == year))
    
}

Identify UC and Cal State Schools

California public universities are identified using mutate() to create a new is_calpublic column in IPEDS that uses str_detect() to identify institution names that contain “California State University” or “University of California.

Note: In order to correctly identify California public schools, it was necessary to include “-” at the end of the University of California string to prevent non-public universities with ‘University of California’ in their name from showing up. For example, “Bethesda University of California” is included in the data set but isn’t apart of either the UC or CSU systems.

# identify 5 schools with largest decline in white students from 2010 to 2020
white_decline <- IPEDS |>
  filter(
    level %in% c("all undergrad", "all graduate"), 
    year %in% c(2010, 2020)
  ) |>
  mutate(
    total_enrollment = rowSums(across(starts_with("enrollment_")), na.rm = TRUE),
    total_white_enrollment = rowSums(across(starts_with("enrollment_") & ends_with("whit")), na.rm = TRUE)
  ) |>
  group_by(institution_id, institution_name, state, year) |>
  summarize(
    school_total_enrollment = sum(total_enrollment),
    school_total_white_enrollment = sum(total_white_enrollment),
    .groups = "drop"
  ) |>
  mutate(percent_white = school_total_white_enrollment / school_total_enrollment) |>
  filter(school_total_enrollment >= 1000) |>

Diversity Still Alive and Well in College Park?

by President Darryll J. Pines, University of Maryland, College Park

The diversity boom of the Obama years has faded and some say its momentum was dampened further with the relatively recent Supreme Court decision in the case of Students for Fair Admissions v. Harvard that removes race as a factor in the college admission process. When faced with this new world of admissions, it led us to wonder, did race-conscious practices prop up our diverse admissions make up or does it make little difference? As a campus dedicated to bringing together myriad voices and visions that usher in a brighter future, we took on the job of checking if we’ve delivered on our promise to our students starting with the two main hallmarks of diversity on campus: gender and race.

Looking Back on 2024

Focusing on the present, in the 2024 academic year we had a total of 41,725 students enrolled at both the undergraduate and graduate levels. Broken down further by gender, we found an identical gender split across levels with both undergraduate and graduate populations being 49% women and 51% men. At the outset, it appears our balance between genders successfully mimics the outside world’s demographics. A peek through the lens of race and ethnicity, though, gives us a much more nuanced story.

Our undergraduate student body comprised mostly of students who were White (38.5%), Asian (23.6%), Black (12.9%) and Hispanic (11.1%). When compared to the previous year (2023), we see that enrollment in every demographic group increased except for White students, who declined by -0.9%. If we zoom in further and look at the first year undergraduate class, we see quite a different story.

After excluding two outlier groups with extremely small student numbers (American Indian/AK Native and Native Hawaiian/Pacific Islander), enrollment has declined in most groups. The only groups that saw a modest increase in students were Hispanics (7.2%), two or more races, and unknown race. The largest decline was in Asian students (-18.5%), followed by Non-Resident and Black students (-7.6% and -7.4%, respectively). In a post-SFFA world, these statistics on their face can possibly indicate that our race-conscious efforts were somewhat key drivers to our diversity but this alone is not enough for UMD to decry the death of diversity just yet.

#| warning: false 
#' IPEDS Data for MP#01 - Data starts in 2010
#' This function will acquire and standardize all data for MP#01
#' from IPEDS (https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/datacenter/DataFiles.aspx)

acquire_ipeds_data <- function(start_year=2010, end_year=2024){
    library(tidyverse)
    library(glue)
    
    data_dir <- file.path("data", "mp01")
    
    if(!dir.exists(data_dir)){
        dir.create(data_dir, showWarnings=FALSE, recursive=TRUE)
    }
    
    YEARS <- seq(start_year, end_year)
    
    EFA_ALL <- map(YEARS, function(yy){
        if(yy <= 2022){
            ef_url <- glue("https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/datacenter/data/EF{yy}A.zip")
            
        } else {
            ef_url <- glue("https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/data-generator?year={yy}&tableName=EF{yy}A&HasRV=0&type=csv")
        }
        
        ef_file <- file.path(data_dir, glue("ef{yy}a.csv.zip"))
        
        if(!file.exists(ef_file)){
            message(glue("Downloading Enrollment Data for {yy} from {ef_url}"))
            download.file(ef_url, destfile = ef_file, quiet=TRUE, mode="wb")    
        }
        
        read_csv(ef_file, 
                 show_col_types=FALSE) |>
            mutate(year = yy, 
                   # American Indian or Alaskan Native
                   enrollment_m_aian = EFAIANM, 
                   enrollment_f_aian = EFAIANW, 
                   # Asian
                   enrollment_m_asia = EFASIAM, 
                   enrollment_f_asia = EFASIAW, 
                   # Black or African-American, 
                   enrollment_m_bkaa = EFBKAAM, 
                   enrollment_f_bkaa = EFBKAAW, 
                   # Hispanic 
                   enrollment_m_hisp = EFHISPM, 
                   enrollment_f_hisp = EFHISPW, 
                   # Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander 
                   enrollment_m_nhpi = EFNHPIM, 
                   enrollment_f_nhpi = EFNHPIW, 
                   # White
                   enrollment_m_whit = EFWHITM, 
                   enrollment_f_whit = EFWHITW, 
                   # Two or More Races
                   enrollment_m_2mor = EF2MORM, 
                   enrollment_f_2mor = EF2MORW, 
                   # Unknown / Undisclosed Race
                   enrollment_m_unkn = EFUNKNM, 
                   enrollment_f_unkn = EFUNKNW, 
                   # US Non-Resident
                   enrollment_m_nral = EFNRALM, 
                   enrollment_f_nral = EFNRALW, 
            ) |> filter(
                (EFALEVEL %in% c(2, 12)) | (LINE %in% c(1, 15))
                # Per 2024 Data Dictionary, 
                # - EFALEVEL 2 = undergrad 
                # - EFALELVE 12 = grad
                # - Line 1 = first year first time full-time undergrad
                # - Line 15 = first year first time part-time undergrad
            ) |> mutate(level = case_when(
                       EFALEVEL == 2 ~ "all undergrad", 
                       EFALEVEL == 12 ~ "all graduate",
                       LINE %in% c(1, 15) ~ "first year undergrad"
                   )
            ) |>
            select(institution_id = UNITID, 
                   year, 
                   level,
                   starts_with("enrollment_")) |>
            group_by(institution_id, 
                     year, 
                     level) |>
            summarize(across(starts_with("enrollment_"), sum), 
                      .groups = "drop")
        
    }) |> bind_rows()
    
    DESC_ALL <- map(YEARS, function(yy){
        if(yy <= 2022){
            hd_url <- glue("https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/datacenter/data/HD{yy}.zip")
            
        } else {
            hd_url <- glue("https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/data-generator?year={yy}&tableName=HD{yy}&HasRV=0&type=csv")
        }
        
        hd_file <- file.path(data_dir, glue("hd{yy}.csv.zip"))
        
        if(!file.exists(hd_file)){
            message(glue("Downloading Institutional Descriptions for {yy} from {hd_url}"))
            download.file(hd_url, destfile = hd_file, quiet=TRUE, mode="wb")    
        }
        
        suppressWarnings(
            read_csv(hd_file, 
                 show_col_types=FALSE, 
                 locale=locale(encoding=if_else(yy==2024, "utf-8", "windows-1252"))) |>
            mutate(year = yy, 
                   INSTNM) |> 
            select(institution_id = UNITID, 
                   institution_name = INSTNM, 
                   state = STABBR, 
                   year)
        )
        
    }) |> bind_rows()
    
    inner_join(EFA_ALL, 
               DESC_ALL, 
               join_by(institution_id == institution_id, 
                       year == year))
    
}

### Diversity Still Alive and Well in College Park?

*by President Darryll J. Pines, University of Maryland, College Park*

The diversity boom of the Obama years has faded and some say its momentum was dampened further with the relatively recent Supreme Court decision in the case of [Students for Fair Admissions v. Harvard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Students_for_Fair_Admissions_v._Harvard) that removes race as a factor in the college admission process. When faced with this new world of admissions, it led us to wonder, did race-conscious practices prop up our diverse admissions make up or does it make little difference? As a campus dedicated to bringing together myriad voices and visions that usher in a brighter future, we took on the job of checking if we've delivered on our promise to our students starting with the two main hallmarks of diversity on campus: gender and race. 

#### Looking Back on 2024 

Focusing on the present, in the 2024 academic year we had a total of `r comma(sum(umd_enrollment_2024$total_enrollment))` students enrolled at both the undergraduate and graduate levels. Broken down further by gender, we found an identical gender split across levels with both undergraduate and graduate populations being `r percent(umd_undergrad_2024$percent_female)` women and `r percent(umd_undergrad_2024$percent_male)` men. At the outset, it appears our balance between genders successfully mimics the outside world's demographics. A peek through the lens of race and ethnicity, though, gives us a much more nuanced story. 

Our undergraduate student body comprised mostly of students who were White (`r percent(race_percents_2024["whit"], accuracy = 0.1)`), Asian (`r percent(race_percents_2024["asia"], accuracy = 0.1)`), Black (`r percent(race_percents_2024["bkaa"], accuracy = 0.1)`) and Hispanic (`r percent(race_percents_2024["hisp"], accuracy = 0.1)`). When compared to the previous year (2023), we see that enrollment in every demographic group increased except for White students, who declined by `r percent(percent_change_undergrad["White"], accuracy = 0.1)`. If we zoom in further and look at the first year undergraduate class, we see quite a different story.

#### Trends Out West

The California public university system has eschewed affirmative action since 1996 when [California Proposition 209](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1996_California_Proposition_209) was passed. In theory, the 2023 Supreme Court decision should have no major bearing on their enrollment numbers making the California public university system a suitable bellwether to compare our enrollment against. If we take a moment to compare UMD's changes in enrollment to those at UC Berkeley, the overall picture paints a somewhat grim trend.

A few of our groups beat out Berkeley's numbers, including Hispanics (UMD: `r percent(percent_change_first_year["Hispanic"], accuracy = 0.1)` , UCB: `r percent(percent_change_ucb_first_year["Hispanic"], accuracy = 0.1)` ), Non-Residents (UMD: `r percent(percent_change_first_year["Non-Resident"], accuracy = 0.1)`, UCB: `r percent(percent_change_ucb_first_year["Non-Resident"], accuracy = 0.1)`) and Unknowns (UMD: `r percent(percent_change_first_year["Unknown"], accuracy = 0.1)`, UCB: `r percent(percent_change_ucb_first_year["Unknown"], accuracy = 0.1)`). In all other groups, UMD enrollment was worse, particularly with Asian students (UMD: `r percent(percent_change_first_year["Asian"], accuracy = 0.1)`, UCB: `r percent(percent_change_ucb_first_year["Asian"], accuracy = 0.1)`). Changes in White student enrollment across both colleges were similar and small with UCB at `r percent(percent_change_ucb_first_year["White"], accuracy = 0.1)` to UMD's `r percent(percent_change_first_year["White"], accuracy = 0.1)` indicating no major advantage for that student group.

#### Comfort in Retrospect

While it may seem that this is a harbinger of worse news down the road, it's important to look back to see if we're on track with our own historical trends. We've tracked a diversity score that indicates the proportion of non-white/non-asian students in our undergraduate classes. Looking at this score, we can breathe a little easier as it has steadily increased for the last 15 years from `r percent(diversity_by_year["2010"], accuracy = 0.1)` in 2010 to `r percent(diversity_by_year["2024"], accuracy = 0.1)` in 2024 and even increased between 2023 and 2024  by `r percent(diversity_change_by_year["2024"], accuracy = 0.1)`. 

Along with this, historical data from 2010 to 2024 shows that a large majority of groups have seen upward trends in enrollment except for White students (down `r percent(largest_enrollment_decrease$percent_change, accuracy = 0.1)`) and the traditional outliers. White enrollment follows a greater downward trend long-term with numbers decreasing even after SFFA.

Taking race out of the equation in admissions may negatively impact our Asian demographic long term but we have evidence that diversity is still alive and thriving in College Park with a shift in the makeup of other minority groups. It appears that SFFA will not be the thread that easily unravels what has long been interwoven into our campus culture.
